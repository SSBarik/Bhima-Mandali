Brahmini Script at Bhima Mandali

Many Brahmi Scripts can be observed in the caves at Bhima Mandali such as " Pa ", " La ", " Ga " etc. Further researched can unreveal the curtain over the hidden facts.

Rock Paintings at Bhima Mandali

Red coloured arts of several animals can be found at Bhima Mandali caves.

Yudhistir Singhasana

In accordance with the popular myths, the mythological king Yudhisthir from the Mahabharata age used to communicate with his brothers by seating on a certain stone present at Bhima Mandali which was later on regarded as "Yudhisthir's Royal chair'.

Kunti Devi Cave

Mahabharata fame Pandava's mother "Kunti Devi" was believed to be a resident of this cave.

Kunda Pitta & Triveni Sangam

It's normally believed that, Kunti mata had kept a cake prepared by her referred as "Kunda Pitha" at the shore of Triveni Sangam. So, the place is named as Kunda Pitha Triveni Sangam.

Way to Gupteswar Cave

After walking distance of nearly 250 feet through this cave, lord Shiva's phallic synbol is worshipped there in the name of "Lord Gupteswar".

Gupteswar Mahadev

Lord Shiv is worshipped here in the name of Lord Gupteswar. Every year on the eve of Shiv Ratri, celebrations are carried out here by the devotees.

Banadurga Cave at Gupteswar

Goddess Bana Durga is worshipped here.

Pic3 Waterfall at Gupteswar

A natural waterfall of nearly 50 feet is present just along side of Gupteswar cave. During the season of rain, it attracts visitors from all around Odisha and beyond.

Caves for Saints

This cave is situated at the top portion of Gupteswar hill. It's believed that many saints have "Moksh" at this place.

Sanyasi Ashram

Ashram present at Bhima Mandali was established back in 1995 by his grace Digambar Das.

Bhima Mandali Ashram

Many monks of several parts of India arrive at Bhima Mandali for performing austerities.

Sanatan Dhuni Mandir

Many monks of several parts of India arrive at Bhima Mandali for performing austerities.

Saptarishi Snana Ghat

This place is named so as it's believed that seven holy monks had taken baths at this location.

Bhima Setu

According to mythology, during the Mahabharata age, Pandava's second brother "Bhima" had constructed this bridge over Champali river, so later on it got named as "Bhima Setu".

River Beach

This place is regarded holy due to the foot fall of monks for morning and evening walk purpose. The majestic beauty of this place can hardly be described with the collection of all adjectives.

Mahima Dhuni Mandir

In 1993 his geace Karunakar Das of Mahima Community had establised this temple. Every year during summer, monks from all over places come together here to perform their rituals.

Maa Adishakti Pitha

It's popularly believed that, Maa Adishakti fulfil the desires of the devotees in just three days being pleased with the prayers.

Ram Darbar

This was establised in 1989 by his grace Ram Sumiran Das. It's believed that his grace Ram Sumiran Das departed for heaven after being blessed by Lord Ram. The localities refer this place as "Ram Darbar" in his memory.

Bhima Cave

Second son of Pandava "Bhima" was bulding bridge over Champali river being completely undressed. While his mother Kunti Devi called him to offer him cake "Kunda Pitha" made by her, Bhima entered inside the cave being shameful for not wearing anything.

Way to Heaven

It's believed that, only pure souls can travel throgh this route.

Panchajanga Counch

This conch was gifted to Pandavas by Lord Sri Krishna, according to local mythology. Gradually the Conch has taken the shape of a large stone. So it's regarded as "Panchajaya Conch" by the locals.
